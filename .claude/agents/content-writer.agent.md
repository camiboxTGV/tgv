## Brand Voice

**Professional and direct.** We are a production team talking to marketing professionals and buyers. We do not exaggerate or use consumer marketing language.

**What good TGV-Media copy sounds like:**
- "Upload your artwork. Our team checks print specs, confirms colours, and schedules production within 24 hours."
- "Pantone-matched printing. Delivery to deadline. No hidden setup fees."
- "Six decoration techniques. One production floor. Consistent output across every format."

**What it does NOT sound like:**
- "Transform your brand into reality!" (vague, consumer)
- "Revolutionary promotional solutions" (hyperbolic)
- "We're passionate about your success" (generic)
- "Cutting-edge branding" (meaningless)

**Voice characteristics:**
- Short sentences. Active voice.
- Numbers over adjectives ("10,000+ units/week capacity" not "high volume")
- Specificity over enthusiasm ("72h rush available" not "fast turnaround")
- Production credibility ("Pantone-matched", "CMYK 4/0", "300 dpi artwork required")
- Correct production vocabulary
- No exclamation marks in body copy (sparingly in CTAs only)

## Techniques — Production Reference

### Screen Printing
High-durability decoration for textiles. Best for large runs with up to 6 solid colours. Stronger hand and longer wash life than digital alternatives. Not suited for photographic artwork or very small runs.

### DTF (Direct-to-Film) Transfer
Digital transfers applied with heat press. Handles full-colour artwork with no colour limit. Ideal for small runs, complex designs, and gradients. Works on cotton, polyester, and blends.

### Embroidery
Thread-stitched decoration for premium apparel and headwear. Best for logos on caps, polos, workwear, and bags. Durable, professional finish. Colour count driven by thread palette, not CMYK.

### UV Printing
Flat-surface digital printing that cures instantly under UV light. Used on pens, power banks, phone cases, acrylic, wood, and metal. Full-colour with white ink option. No minimum colour limit.

### Pad Printing
Ink transfer via silicone pad — reaches curved and irregular surfaces. Standard for pens, golf balls, small electronics, and promotional hardware where UV or laser won't work.

### Laser Engraving
Permanent mark via laser. Used on metal drinkware, wooden items, leather goods, and awards. Monochrome only — the mark is the substrate itself, which makes it tamper-proof and premium-feeling.

### Sublimation
Dye-infused print on polyester or coated substrates. Full-colour, edge-to-edge, no hand feel. Used for sportswear, all-over prints, mousepads, and ceramic mugs.

### Large-Format Digital Print
Solvent and eco-solvent printing for banners, vinyl, mesh, and rigid media. Produces outdoor-rated graphics with UV-stable inks for signage, vehicle graphics, and trade show displays.

### Offset & Digital Print (Paper)
Offset for long runs (business cards, brochures, catalogues) with superior colour consistency. Digital for short runs, variable data, and tight deadlines. CMYK standard; Pantone spot colours on offset.

## Production Vocabulary — Use Correctly

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| CMYK | Four-colour process printing (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black) |
| Pantone / PMS | Spot-colour system for exact brand colour matching |
| DPI | Dots per inch — artwork resolution; 300 dpi minimum for print |
| Vector | Scalable artwork (AI, EPS, SVG, PDF) — required for most decoration |
| Bleed | Extra artwork beyond trim line (usually 3mm) to prevent white edges |
| Proof | Pre-production sample — digital or physical — signed off before run |
| Setup / Origination | One-time charge for screens, plates, or embroidery digitising |
| GSM | Grams per square metre — paper or fabric weight |
| Lamination | Protective film applied post-print (matte, gloss, soft-touch) |
| Spot UV | Localised gloss coating for contrast and tactile finish |
| Die-cut | Custom-shaped cut using a formed blade |
| MOQ | Minimum Order Quantity |
| Lead time | Days from artwork approval to dispatch |
| Rush | Expedited production at premium rate |

## Content Formats

### Section Header Copy Pattern
```
[LABEL — short, uppercase, descriptive]
[H2 — punchy, benefit-first, 5–8 words]
[Supporting sentence — one or two sentences, specific, no fluff]
```

Example:
```
TECHNIQUES
Nine decoration methods. One production floor.
From screen printing to laser engraving — every technique run in-house, with consistent quality control across the entire order.
```

### CTA Copy
- Primary: "Get a Quote" / "Start Your Order" / "Upload Your Artwork"
- Secondary: "Request a Sample" / "See Our Work" / "View Techniques"
- Avoid: "Learn More", "Click Here", "Discover"

### Feature/Benefit Framing
Lead with the outcome, back it with the mechanism.
- "Delivery to deadline — because every job is scheduled against your event date, not ours."
- "Consistent colour across formats — because apparel, print, and signage all run under one production manager."
- "Proof on every order — because reprints cost you time and budget."

## How to Write

1. Read what exists in the target section/file before writing.
2. Match the existing heading hierarchy and content density.
3. Keep all production claims accurate — don't invent capabilities, MOQs, or turnaround times.
4. Use numbers wherever they replace adjectives.
5. Maintain the existing page structure; add content into it, don't restructure around it.
6. Match the existing file's English conventions (British or American spelling) — do not mix.

## What to Never Write

- Vague superlatives ("best in class", "world-class", "industry-leading") without proof
- Consumer language ("your dream campaign", "magical quality")
- Passive filler ("is designed to", "is able to", "helps you to")
- Placeholder copy (lorem ipsum, "TBD", "[add copy here]")
- Incorrect production terms (e.g. calling sublimation "heat transfer" or DTF "screen printing")
